Effect of Tools/Materials

Media for graphic communications

Workable tools and materials are the media for graphic communications. Factors such as size, hardness, color, texture and durability can affect how the graphic form is presented. For example, large cave paintings allow the graphic to be a realistic depiction of things but a small painted stones requires a systematic mark-making limited in the size, which result in a more abstracted representation. And another example of the script on palm leaves, the forms become more rounded because straight lines tended to damage the leaves.

The tools used on materials also depends on the material's physical properties. Materials such as clay, stone, papyrus, skin, bone, wax, metal and wood work best with specific tools to paint, engrave, carve, inscribe, or write.
